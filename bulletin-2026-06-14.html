## Reflection

In today's Gospel, Jesus sends his disciples out to minister to a troubled and weary world because "the harvest is abundant but the labourers are few." Christ still looks upon humanity with compassion. He still sees people wandering like sheep without a shepherd. Through our baptism, he calls each of us to bring our unique gifts into service for a world that hungers for God's love. But how do we do that? Isn't ministry something for professionals — priests, religious, theologians, parish leaders? What could I possibly offer?

![img-0.jpeg](img-0.jpeg)

The beauty of Christian service is that everyone is called to become Christ for others. We each carry unique gifts, life experiences, wounds, and moments of grace that Jesus can use to bring healing and invitation to those who feel troubled or abandoned. Christian ministry does not begin with expertise. It begins with encounter. It begins with our relationship with Christ. In the Gospel, Jesus calls his disciples by name before sending them out on mission. He knows each of them personally. He knows their strengths and weaknesses, their fears and failings. Yet he calls them anyway. The world is still full of troubled and abandoned souls searching for hope. And Christ still walks among the crowds with compassion. May we open our hearts to hear him call us by name, so that through our words, our kindness, our prayers and our service, his lost sheep may finally find their way home.

# Day for Life

On this year's Day for Life, Sunday 21st June, we reflect on the wonder of human life from the moment of conception. We remember how the LORD Jesus Christ Himself sanctified and experienced the beginning of life in the world as an unborn child, hidden in the womb of Mary. Mary knew that she was carrying the Son of God – God and man – as soon as He was conceived. John the Baptist leapt in his mother Elizabeth's womb when Mary greeted her. Our Lady treasured Our Lord in her heart and womb until His birth. To read the full bishops' message visit www.councilforlife.ie

# CELEBRATING ST. EUNAN'S CATHEDRAL 125th ANNIVERSARY

Day in and day out, every day of the year, from before dawn in the short days of winter to the going down of the sun in the long days of summer, our beloved Cathedral is filled with prayer. The quiet prayer of that place where each of us finds his place; the candle lit in sickness, distress and the tests and challenges of life; the prayer with others as we praise the name of Mary, of the Saints, with the Scriptures and song and candlelight. And above all the prayer which is the prayer of Christ, the prayer which renders all prayer effective, the prayer by which he offers himself once for all but eternally present to the Father on the cross, the prayer that is filled with the light of the resurrection, the source of all grace, the sum of every sacrifice, the sacrament of love, the Holy Eucharist: the Mass. The very stones in this place are saturated with the prayer of this and every generation, a worthy echo of the prayer offered on hillside and cabin and sheltered glen where even the little that clothed the back and fed the hungry was risked for love of Christ by those who were living stones making a spiritual house to the praise and glory of God the Father.

When the ages of oppression and marginalisation had passed and the people had found a footing with greater confidence, our forebears wished to raise a temple that would be worthy of God, of the Holy Mass and of their love for Him in long centuries of sacrifice. Their shepherd Bishop O'Donnell was suited in temperament and ability to their desire to have a cathedral that would honour the saints and scholars whose memories were still revered. Under his drive and direction, priests and peoples were united in one aim: to give the best, to aim for the highest achievement, to spare no effort that God might be praised and the people might have a church in which to gather with every solemnity and beauty.

But while this place has been further beautified in the intervening years, and while solemn music of great beauty has shaken rafters and moved hearts, with the grace of the sacraments and the preaching of the word, the greatest beauty of this place has been the beauty hoped for but which is the gift of God alone to noble hearts: the grace of faith that expresses itself in profound love of God flowing out in acts of love and charity in homes and neighbourhood and town,

Page 8

in foreign lands and mission fields. It is for the grace of faith that we pray today, were every stone and all its beauty pass from this promontory which holds the pinnacle and jewel of our town, that still the greater beauty of holiness would still shine forth, give its shelter to seeking souls and honour to God most high. At the last, may each of us find ourselves in the eternal City, the New Jerusalem, where there is no temple other than God himself, and where there is no need for sun and moon, since the Lamb is the light of that City. May our brothers and sisters, our fathers and mothers who have passed to that place pray for us, that what God has begun in us from that holy place may bring us there in the completion of eternal life.
